      Rules summary

      • Coop mode follows Codenames: Duet: 25 words, two private key sides, 15 shared agents, timer tokens, and sudden death.
      • For coop one-device play, place a paper screen on the center line before revealing the opposite-facing key sides.
      • For coop multi-device play, scan Side A and Side B QR codes to show private keys on separate phones.
      • Compete mode follows standard Codenames: two teams use one shared spymaster key, with the starting team having 9 agents and the other team having 8.
      • For compete one-device play, guessing players should turn around, cover their eyes, or step away before the spymasters reveal the shared key.
      • The competitive one-device key display includes a countdown timer that can be adjusted in 30-second steps, paused, continued, and reset.
      • For compete multi-device play, scan the spymaster QR code to show the shared key on a separate phone.
      • Solo mode creates one hidden key with 15 agents, 7 bystanders, and 3 assassins. Copy the role string to an AI chatbot, import CSV clues with associated contact words, then guess from the board.
      • In either mode, a clue is one word plus a number. Guessers must make at least one guess, then may stop if the turn is still active.
      • In compete mode, your team's agent lets you keep guessing, a bystander ends the turn, an opponent agent scores for the opponent and ends the turn, and the assassin makes your team lose immediately.
      • In coop mode, a green guess is contacted and guessing may continue. A bystander ends the turn. An assassin loses immediately.
      • In coop sudden death, no clues are given: contact all remaining agents, but any non-agent guess loses.

      Based on the official Codenames rulebook and official Codenames: Duet rulebook.
